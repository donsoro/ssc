The visit of existing and functioning early warning systems in Kenya has allowed to develop the capacities of national structures responsible for the establishment of early warning systems. The acquired knowledge allowed for  a transitioning early warning system to manage the 2014 floods. This has significantly improved  the national coverage  ( by 26% from 30 to 56%) for climate monitoring  and data collection (to a daily basis)
